        Finally convinced the hubs to get outa the house yesterday and grab a bite. I've had this place booked marked for forever but we always seem to be in the mood to hit it on Sundays but their closed.  

        Located in the heart of down town Willow Glen, this traditional Italian deli is a local landmark that everyone near and far knows about. Parking can be a challenge on the weekends to be prepared.

        We were lucky enough to find a parking spot in the lot behind the shop. Score! They have a few tables to sit at outside by the street and a covered area in the back if the lot. The tables by the street were packed which should have warned us for what we encountered next.

        As we opened the Deli door .... BAM! The line .... I swear I swear there were 80 hungry customers waiting inside this itty bitty shop. The hubs was not happy cuz he hates to wait for anything. But .... Tuff luck buddy, we're here and we're staying. They use the pull tab numbering system so we pulled ours and began our wait. Unfortunately since it was so crowded you could hardly move, I wasn't able to enjoy the culinary delights they had scattered around. However one thing was perfectly clear, EVERYONE comes for the raviolis. You can buy them fresh made and frozen to cook at home or they have a case with daily hot specials which included ribs, meatballs, ravioli, pizza, stuffed shells, lasagna, roast chicken, rice balls and a handful of other yummy options.

        After waiting 30 minutes, it was our turn. We ordered the secret sandwich which really isn't a secret cuz everyone (even the entire 49er football team knows about it and orders it) called the "Chris Combo". We also order on meatball and one meat stuffed aricini rice ball.

        Chris Combo - served warm on a super soft yet hearty roll. The flavors were excellent and each bite was great! There was something that gave off a little heat but not super spicy for sure.

        Meatball - tender, flavorful and big!

        Arancini - make sure they warm it for you because I didn't thinking I could pop it in the oven at home which I did but it took forever to warm because its so large and dense. But boy was it yummy! Rice ball filled with meat, veg and cheeeeeeese. A tad pricey at over $4 but tasty for sure.

        Ill absolutely be back to try the raviolis and some of the other homemade, mouthwatering goodness.

        I got some ravioli and lasagna for my birthday meal because Italian food is not something I eat on a regular basis. But when I do, it's the most delicious meal I look forward to, and I savor it with every ounce of life I have in me.

        The food was a complete success. The must have is the ravioli and lasagna, both of which I got and both of which were utterly perfect. Get me a nice red and I am yours forever.

        The ravioli tastes very fresh. The skin is homemade I'm sure, because it was so soft and at the same time, chewy. Really great. The lasagna was made with mozz, and ricotta. Meat sauce was not too overwhelming and the tangy tomato sauce was perfection.

        The taste of the meat sauce was comforting and rich and so incredibly wonderful. I'm so glad this type of restaurant exists in San Jose and everyone can enjoy high quality food as a casual to-go place. There's nothing I would want to change about this place aside from opening on Sunday's.

        It's situated in a cute neighborhood of willow glen and its patrons are as loyal as the ravioli makers themselves. Really fresh and homemade, you can't really find anything even relatively similar in San Jose for the same prices. I paid about $23, for a quart sized jar of ravioli and a large piece of lasagna with garlic bread.

        I should go back more often. This store is truly something special. Please don't ever stop what you're doing!
