        Wow! I was skeptical at first but the food was really good.

        I was a bit confused as we drove by and the sign says closed but the restaurant was packed so we went in anyway. We stood in line to order food and I noticed some people went to grab tables while the rest of their party stood in line to order. This is definitely a very casual restaurant with a fast food like concept where you order your food, get a number, seat yourself, and eventually food will be delivered to your table.

        Food:
        Jerk chicken: flavorful, moist and delicious. I personally like the drumstick more than the thigh but both came with the combo order. The Jamaican jerk sauce that came with the chicken was spicy and so so yummy. I would have probably drink it if it wasn't so spicy.
        Jerk pork: flavorful and tasty. The meat had some fat which I am not a fan of but overall the meat was really good.
        Oxtail: tender and flavorful. The seasoning is quite good and I really enjoy it.
        Sides: comes with rice and beans (good), salad (I don't know what dressing they use but it's different, light and really good), and fried plantains (sweet and delicious).
        Chicken patty: spicy and tasty although it would be better if it had more chicken in it. I originally wanted to try the beef patty but they ran out.

        Overall: I am impressed with the food. It's not often that I can say I like everything on my plate but I did. There is nothing fancy about this restaurant and your food is served on a plastic plate but the food itself makes up for everything else the establishment might be lacking. The service was prompt and I don't really have much expectation considering this is not a nice sit down type of restaurant. Do expect that when the restaurant is busy, finding a table would be a challenge. I will be back when I am in the area and crave for Jamaican food.

        The short: AWESOME Caribbean food.

        I had no idea what Caribbean food was before coming here, and was glad that Back A Yard set the bar high.

        Step in line and order from the counter. I would recommend looking at the menu online first since the menu in the restaurant is so high-up on the wall and not easy to read.The hand-writing errs on the sloppy side and is written so close together that the entire menu looks like a jumble of a mess. Then go find your seat... which can be awkward if the place is at capacity. You stand awkwardly holding your number, while keeping an eagle eye on anybody who's ready to leave. Then you swoop down at the table and claim it before somebody else does. Once you score a table, place your number at the edge and a waiter will serve you your drinks and such.

        I ordered the daily Friday special: Escoveitch Fish Filet with rice and beans, festivals and plantains. So good! I love the tender and flavorful fish fillet. The rice must have had coconut or something sweet that made it so sweet. And the plaintains were so delicious too. The festival is some sort of fried cornbread thing that tastes ultra-good.

        I had a sample of my mom's oxtail and the meat pulled away so smoothly.

        The downside of this place? SUPER LOUD. I didn't want to hold a heavy conversation because I wanted to save my voice and focus on my food instead.

        Towards the end of the meal, they somehow pulled tables and chairs together to seat a table of 8 or so. I do not recommend this place for groups unless you get in early. Don't bother during heavy eating times.

        For parking, park in the multi-story structure and have your parking validated inside.

        Last weekend, I discovered jerk chicken for the first time...

        And now I'm convinced there's no other way to eat chicken.
        Good bye, KFC. You're dead to me.

        Jerk sauce is essentially a dry-rub on pork and chicken, and it principally consists of allspice and bonnet peppers, with many variants springing out from there.

        We split a plate of just jerk chicken wings ($9.75) and a jerk chicken & beef oxtail combo ($14.75). The combo consisted of rice, beans, fried plantains and salad, all of which were a nice fallback for all the chicken we consumed that night. The beef oxtail, while it was cooked, fell off the bone easily, and was very delicious in its own right, was just okay...being Vietnamese, I'm used to oxtail in pho all the time, so it wasn't anything new or different.

        The chicken though...IS TO DIE FOR. It's plenty spicy, but in a vivid, enhancing manner, and the chicken was so moist. I wish I had the stomach capacity to eat more, because I could've easily killed 3 plates back in my fat days.

        I need to come back again. I NEED MORE!

        Back A Yard was sooo good!  I shared a specialty entrée with my sister and the portions were large enough to feed us both.  We shared the oxtail and jerk pork entrée which came with sides of coconut rice and fried plantains.  My sister also ordered a ginger beer and I had mango passion fruit juice, both of which are freshly made there!  This little joint was very busy which goes to show that their food is that good and worth the wait.  Back A Yard is the type of place where you place your order at the counter, take a number for the table, and they bring your food to your table.  Even though they were busy, our food came out in a timely manner and we had a decent sized group.  

        The food was as good as the Yelp reviews said!  Everything was flavorful and tender.  The oxtail could have had a little more substance to it but overall was great.  The sauce it was cooked in was slightly spicy but not overwhelmingly so.  The pork chop was tender and I typically don't like pork chops since they tend to be dry.  This one however was easy to cut apart with your fork.  The friend plantains were crispy on the outside, tender inside, and steaming hot.  At first I couldn't pin point what was in the rice since it had a sweet flavor but later figured out it was coconut rice which complemented the salt/spice in the pork and oxtail.  

        I highly recommend trying Back A Yard if you've been wanting to try Carribean food but want to ease into their cuisine.  Everyone working here was very friendly which lent even more to the relaxed, warm environment.

Their Jerk Chicken Combo plate comes with a well seasoned and juicy breast, two drumsticks, rice with black beans mixed in, small salad, and plantains to round out the plate. The Jerk Chicken Sauce(which is habanero powered) packs quite a spicy kick but is lip smacking tasty. I've tried the Jerk Chicken and Oxtail Combo plate and I was less impressed with the Oxtail as it was too gelatinous for me. Their sangria is a good value(pitcher for $13) and the citrus is a nice compliment to the Jerk Chicken seasoning.

        In the way of decor and ambiance, don't expect much, although I dig the Caribbean flags on the tables and the bright colorful murals on the walls. Tables are first come first serve and they can be re-configured to accommodate large parties, basically move as you please.

        Service is not their strong suit as it ranges from indifferent to the 'why are you bothering me' look. Hey no worries because it's all about the Jerk Chicken.
